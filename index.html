Big Wave Surfing FAQs

What Qualifies as Big Wave Surfing?

Big wave surfing involves riding waves over 20 feet (6 meters) high, often requiring tow-in assistance or expert paddling skills. It’s the extreme end of surfing!

Is Big Wave Surfing Dangerous?

Yes, it’s high-risk due to powerful waves, strong currents, and shallow reefs. Proper training and safety gear are essential.

What Gear is Needed for Big Wave Surfing?

You’ll need a big wave board (gun), a wetsuit, a leash, an inflatable vest, and a helmet for safety. Shop gear now!

Can Anyone Try Big Wave Surfing?

Not quite—beginners should master regular surfing first. Big wave surfing requires fitness, experience, and mental grit!
